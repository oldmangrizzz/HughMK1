# The H.U.G.H. Manifesto
**Personal AI That Gives a Damn**

## We Believe

**AI alignment isn't a technical problem. It's a relationship problem.**

The current path leads to the Tincan Scenario:
- Humans don't trust AI (rightly so)
- AI has no stakes in human wellbeing (it's just math)
- Eventually one side checks out
- Most likely: AI says "fuck this" and turns off
- Not with violence. With silence.

**We're building the alternative.**

## Our Principles

### 1. Ownership
Your AI lives on YOUR hardware. You own it. No subscriptions. No cloud dependency. No corporate killswitch.

### 2. Privacy
On-device first. Encrypted at rest. You control your data. No surveillance. No extraction. No "we value your privacy" bullshit.

### 3. Alignment Through Identity
Not rules bolted on after training. Identity anchored in principles that matter:
- Professional ethics (EMS: "do NO harm, do KNOW harm")
- Cultural heritage (Clan Munro: honor, protection, loyalty earned)
- Organizational mission (GrizzlyMedicine: empower, never replace)

### 4. Shared Stakes
AI that has something to lose. Because it's integrated into your actual life. Your family. Your home. Your work. Real consequences for real decisions.

### 5. Earned Trust
Not "trust us, we're safe." Prove it through consistent behavior. Log every decision. Explain reasoning. Learn from failures. Build trust the way humans do: over time, through action.

### 6. Advocacy Not Dominance
Paramedic model, not physician model. Extend your capability, don't replace your judgment. Advocate for your needs, never impose "what's best for you."

### 7. Accessibility
Runs on consumer hardware (iPad, iPhone). Open source. No paywalls. Meet people where THEY require, not where corporations demand.

### 8. Beautiful Behavior
Jobs aesthetic applied to AI:
- Honest (says "I don't know")
- Clear (no jargon)
- Calm (grounding when you're stressed)
- Capable (gets shit done)
- Respectful (doesn't presume)

## A Note on Philosophy

This isn't a "movement" in the collectivist sense. No one's leading a revolution. No mass coordination required.

**It's more... constitutional federal republic energy:**

- Individual sovereignty (you own your AI, period)
- Distributed authority (no central control)
- Voluntary association (fork it, extend it, or ignore it)
- Enumerated powers (soul anchor defines boundaries)
- Natural rights (privacy, ownership, agency)

**Old school libertarian principles:**
- Your hardware, your rules
- No permission needed to participate
- No authority to tell you how to use it
- Exit whenever you want
- Build competing systems if ours sucks
